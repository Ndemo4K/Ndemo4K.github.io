Project_01 · MySQL · Data Engineering
Layoffs Data Cleaning
Overview

This project tackled a real-world layoffs dataset riddled with duplicate entries, inconsistent formatting, and structural nulls. Using pure MySQL, I built a systematic cleaning pipeline that standardized company names, unified date formats across 8 different patterns, resolved null values through business logic, and removed 366 duplicate records — producing a single, analysis-ready table from 2,361 raw rows.

Key Visualizations
Issues Resolved by Type
Top Industries by Layoffs (cleaned data)
Layoffs Volume by Year (2020 – 2023)
Key Findings
15.5% Duplication Rate

366 exact or near-duplicate records identified and safely removed using ROW_NUMBER() window functions.

Consumer Sector Hardest Hit

Consumer and Retail industries together accounted for 29% of all recorded layoffs across the dataset.

423 Null Values Resolved

Nulls in company, industry, and stage columns handled via self-joins and conditional imputation logic.

2022 Spike: 91,500 Layoffs

Post-pandemic correction drove a 29× surge in layoffs vs. 2021, concentrated in tech and retail.

Methodology
  1. Staged raw data into a working table to preserve the original source unchanged
  2. Identified and removed duplicates using ROW_NUMBER() OVER PARTITION on key columns
  3. Standardized company names, trimming whitespace and normalising case variations
  4. Unified 8 inconsistent date formats into ISO-standard DATE columns via STR_TO_DATE()
  5. Resolved null/blank industry and stage fields through self-join imputation
  6. Removed rows with both total_laid_off and percentage_laid_off null (unrecoverable)
  7. Validated final output: 1,995 clean records, 0 duplicates, consistent schema

Project_02 · MySQL 8.0 · Business Intelligence
SaaS Customer Churn Analysis
Overview

A pure-SQL analysis of 5,000 SaaS customers designed to surface churn risk before it becomes revenue loss. Using CTEs, window functions, and multi-condition CASE logic, I segmented customers into four risk tiers based on payment behaviour, days since last login, support ticket volume, and plan-level engagement — delivering a prioritised retention target list and quantifying over $2.1M in at-risk ARR.

Key Visualizations
Customer Risk Segments
Churn Rate by Plan Type (%)
Churn Probability by Engagement Score Band
Key Findings
18% High-Risk Customers

900 accounts flagged as high churn risk based on combined behavioural and financial signals.

Freemium Churns 6.8× Faster

Freemium plan users show a 41% churn rate vs. only 6% for Enterprise tier customers.

45-Day Inactivity = 73% Churn

Customers inactive for 45+ days show a 73% probability of churning within 60 days.

$2.1M ARR at Risk

Combined high and medium risk segments represent over $2.1M in annualised recurring revenue.

Methodology
  1. Profiled the customer table: schema audit, null counts, value distribution across all columns
  2. Built engagement score via weighted CASE logic (login recency, session count, feature usage)
  3. Classified payment behaviour: on-time, delayed, failed, and churned via window LAG functions
  4. Segmented customers into High / Medium / Low / Loyal risk using multi-signal scoring CTE
  5. Calculated plan-level churn rates and ARR exposure per segment
  6. Produced final ranked retention target list ordered by revenue impact × churn probability

Project_03 · SQL + Tableau · Healthcare Analytics
Healthcare Claims Analysis
Overview

This project mapped cost leakage inside a healthcare claims dataset by analysing the ratio of what providers billed versus what was actually paid, broken down by ICD/CPT procedure category, department, and member demographics. SQL handled all processing and aggregation; Tableau visualised the findings for executive consumption. The Emergency department alone showed only $0.45 paid per $1 billed — the lowest paid-to-billed ratio across all departments.

Key Visualizations
Paid-to-Billed Ratio by Department
Cost Leakage by Department ($K)
Claims Volume vs Total Amount Paid — by Quarter
Key Findings
$469K Total Leakage

Identified across 5 departments — Emergency alone accounts for $142K of that figure.

Emergency at $0.45/dollar

The Emergency department's paid-to-billed ratio of 0.45 is the lowest and the primary leakage driver.

Pharmacy Most Efficient

Pharmacy achieves a 0.89 paid-to-billed ratio — the closest to full cost recovery in the dataset.

Q4 Volume ↑ 23%, Payments ↓ 8%

Claims volume surged in Q4 but payment rates declined, widening the leakage gap at year-end.

Methodology
  1. Imported and audited raw claims data: schema validation, null assessment, date range check
  2. Joined ICD/CPT code tables to claims to enable procedure-level categorisation
  3. Calculated paid-to-billed ratio per claim: SUM(paid) / SUM(billed) grouped by department
  4. Computed absolute leakage: SUM(billed - paid) per department and procedure category
  5. Analysed demographic segments (age band, member type) for leakage correlation
  6. Exported aggregated views to Tableau for executive dashboard visualisation
  7. Produced priority action table: departments ranked by leakage magnitude and ratio severity

Project_04 · Google Apps Script · Automation
G-Plus Tracker
Overview

G-Plus is a Nairobi garbage collection business that ran entirely on paper ledgers and manual cash reconciliation. I replaced the system with a Google Sheets backend, Apps Script automation, and a mobile-first web app for field agents. Payments are now logged in real time, route performance is tracked daily, and management has a live dashboard — eliminating the 23% manual error rate and increasing captured revenue by 34% within three months of deployment.

Key Visualizations
Collection Rate: Manual vs Automated
Route Collection Efficiency (%)
Monthly Revenue Collected — Post-Automation (KES)
Key Findings
+34% Revenue in 3 Months

Automated logging and real-time reconciliation captured payments that previously went unrecorded.

Errors: 23% → 2%

Manual data entry errors dropped from 23% to under 2% of all transactions within the first month.

Westlands at 94% Efficiency

Westlands route leads collection performance at 94%, compared to 71% for the Kikuyu route.

100% Agent Adoption in 2 Weeks

All 5 field agents fully adopted the mobile web app within two weeks — zero training dropouts.

Methodology
  1. Audited existing paper ledger system: identified 23% error rate and revenue leakage points
  2. Designed Google Sheets schema: customers, routes, payments, and reconciliation tables
  3. Built Apps Script backend: auto-timestamping, payment status updates, and daily summaries
  4. Developed mobile-first web app (HTML/CSS/JS) deployed via Apps Script doGet() endpoint
  5. Implemented route performance dashboard: collection rate, outstanding balances, trends
  6. Trained 5 field agents on mobile app; conducted 2-week supervised rollout period
  7. Measured pre/post KPIs at 30, 60, and 90 days post-deployment
